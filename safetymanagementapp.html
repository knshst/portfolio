Safety Management App

Safety Management App is an SaaS application for commercial organizations to ensure employee safety in the event of an emergency or disaster. By automatically sending out questionnaires and collecting responses from employees, the app can provide an accurate summary of the safety status of an organization in real time. It enables organizations to quickly identify potential risks and ensure employee safety while minimizing the disruption of business operations.

Summary

Project Overview

When I took over the project, it had been stagnant for a few months, with 50% of the development already completed. My task was to get the project back on track and finish the development process while adhering to the highest quality standards.

Project Structure

Since our development had been outsourced to a partner vendor located in South Asia, I was the sole member of the cross-functional team based in Japan. As there was no one in the Japan team who could speak English and no supervisor to oversee me, I managed and drove the project remotely from Japan.

HOW I WORKED

Problem Discovery & Study

The fact that the development of the product was stuck at 50% was certainly a problem. However, an even bigger issue was that the project had been moving forward without verifying if there was a market need for it.

So simply, I found out those are the problem in big scope.

・The development was incomplete.

・Not sure if there is a market need for the product.

Solution Proposal & Planning

I have restructured the project plan into four phases with clear goals. These phases are Conducting Customer Research, Building Product Strategy, Development, and Beta Release.

The first point was to investigate whether there was a need for this product in the market, as it didn't make sense to create a product without it. If there was, then the next step was to develop a product strategy which I added to provide the team with direction and to help them proceed with the project strategically and efficiently. If we could ensure the product met the needs, it was time to continue the project and the rest of the development. Once the development and testing phase was done, the product was released as a beta version to assess its market fit instead of immediately releasing it to the world. Again, this beta release was essential to ensure that the product met customer needs and finalized the product.

This enabled us to collect enough data to ensure that the product was fitting the market and customer's needs.

Development

As a product manager, I improved the development process to make it more efficient. I added several steps to the process, which are shown in the before and after images below. The steps highlighted in dark blue are what I took on as a product manager. I gathered product ideas through brainstorming sessions, either on my own or with internal stakeholders, or through customer feedback collected by the sales team as it was a B2B product. I also participated in meetings as pre-sales to hear directly from customers. Additionally, I ran SQL queries to retrieve data and analyze customer behaviour.

AS-IS

In this new development process, I implemented a backlog management system to effectively organize feedback and ideas into a product backlog. I created user stories and requirements for each ticket to ensure a clear understanding for the engineers and designers. I also held weekly stand-up meetings to discuss the next sprint and track progress.If the decision was made to move forward, the ticket was shifted to the sprint backlog. After that, the engineering team conducted technical research to evaluate the feasibility of the project and the technologies to be used.

TO-BE

If development was viable, I constructed a prototype to prevent miscommunication during the design process (You can see what it looks like in the following images). After obtaining internal approval from the executive, we began designing the system and moved on to development. We then conducted a variety of tests to improve product quality such as unit tests, scenario tests, and integration tests. The scenario tests were created and conducted by the product manager in the form of a test specification. After deployment to the production environment, the same tests were conducted again to complete the process.

Beta Release

Upon the release of the product, we established escalation paths for customer inquiries and constructed procedures for handling them.
