Safety Management App

Safety Management App is an essential tool for commercial organizations to ensure employee safety in the event of an emergency or disaster. By automatically sending out questionnaires and collecting responses from employees, the app can provide an accurate summary of the safety status of an organization in real time. It enables organizations to quickly identify potential risks and ensure employee safety while minimizing the disruption of business operations.

Summary

Project Overview

This product was proposed by internal stakeholders and development had already been completed by 50% when I took over the project. However, the project had been stagnant for a few months and the team had become stuck in a situation where they were unsure of what to do next.

Project Structure

Since my company had outsourced our development to a partner vendor located in Bangladesh, all members of the cross-functional team (three engineers and one designer) were based abroad. Since there was nobody who could speak English in my company and no supervisor to oversee me, I had to lead and manage the project remotely from Japan by myself. Those achievements I describe in the following section were all driven solely by me.

HOW I WORKED

【STEP1】Problem Discovery & Study

The fact that the development of the product was stuck at 50% was certainly a problem. However, an even bigger issue was that the project had been moving forward without verifying if there was a market need for it.

So simply, I found out those are the problem in big scope.

・The development was incomplete.

・Not sure if there is a market need for the product.

【STEP2】 Solution Proposal & Planning

To address this issue, I have restructured the project plan into four phases with clear goals. These phases are Conducting Customer Research, Building Product Strategy, Development, and Beta Release.

The first point is to investigate whether there is a need for this product in the market, as it doesn't make sense to create a product without it. If there is, then the next step is to develop a product strategy which I added to provide the team with direction and to help them proceed with the project strategically and efficiently. If we can ensure the product meets the needs, we can continue the project and the rest of the development should be completed. Once the development phase is completed, the product should be released as a beta version to assess its market fit instead of immediately releasing it to the world. Again, this beta release is essential to ensure that the product meets customer needs and finalizes the product.

【STEP3】 Execution

Customer Research

Since this product was still in the development stage and we had no information or metric on how customers would interact with it, I decided first to get feedback from our employees in the General Affairs department, which is the target audience for this product in the EMUC framework. I also utilized existing customers by offering the product for free use and collecting their feedback, as gathering feedback from prospects or new customers would take a long time and be more difficult. This enabled us to collect enough data to ensure that the product was fitting the market and customer's needs.

Product Strategy

  2. Define Target and Meet Needs
